HIERARCHY

identity is 1:m to motion 
motion is m:1 or m:0 to identity

identity can define motion but it cannot uniquely predict motion. motion can infer identity but it cannot prove identity. 

a tiger is not a tiger because it runs fast; it runs fast because it's a tiger

objectivity is dominated by downward constraints
subjectivity is where local activation dominates 

identity-first: ontological containment and definition
motion-first: generative and causal production

parent: objective relative to child
child: subjective relative to parent

---

Identity-first objectivity:
Parent defines stable classification boundaries.

Motion-first objectivity:
Parent is generative condition for child existence.

Subjectivity under identity-first:
Child depends on parent for category meaning.

Subjectivity under motion-first:
Child depends on parent for causal production.

child -> parent (update)
parent -> children (cascade)

motion dominance patterns:

* States change constraint motion
* Constraint motion determines reliability
* Reliability differences feel like objective vs subjective
* Learning direction determines cascade risk
* Hierarchy position determines relational objectivity

dreams and psychedelics shift motion dominance
